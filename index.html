Brief History

Before it was known as Angeles City, it was just a little community commanded by Don Angel Pantalion de Miranda, Captain Mayor of San Fernando. It was given the name "Culiat" after a woody vine that grew abundantly in the area at the time. The Negrito Tribesman were the people who lived there.General Frederick D. Grant founded the first American administration of Angles on January 1, 1900, and Angeles City became a city 64 years later.

Barrio Culiat remained a part of San Fernando for 33 years, during which time a church, primary school, and certain industries, such as a sugar mill and wine distillery, were established, allowing the barrio's economy to grow. After Don Angel paid the appropriate sum required by the legislation at the time for the separation of Culiat, the barrio became a distinct town from San Fernando on December 8, 1829. The town's name was then changed to "El Pueblos de los Angeles" in honor of its patron saint, the Holy Guardian Angels, as well as Don Angel, the town's founder.

As of 2010, the population of Angeles City was 326,336. The expected growth rate is 2.32 percent. Kapampangan is the official language, however Tagalog and English are also spoken and understood across the province.

Angeles City is located in the Philippines' Central Luzon area, in the province of Pampanga. The city is steeped in history and heritage, with old and historically significant structures dotting the landscape, but it is best known these days for its large concentration of casinos and active nightlife for men.

Toponyms

  • Sto. Rosario

    Barangay Sto. Rosario is home to art galleries, shopping malls, supermarkets, restaurants, and a university. Sto. Rosario is a blend of historical and contemporary frameworks in Angeles City. The Angeles Museum, the Pamintuan Mansion, Don Angel Panteleon de Miranda's mansion (founding member of Angeles City), the Center for Kapampangan Studies, and the Holy Rosary Parish Church are on one side, and malls such as Nepo Mall, Newpoint, Jenra, and numerous eateries and low-cost product retail chains are on the other. Because of this one-stop shopping situation, it became well-known, particularly among Holy Angel University students, as a location to unwind after school.

  • Crossing

    Do you have a strong desire for BBQ and other sorts of street food? Because of the variety of barbeque and grilling businesses around, Crossing is a popular gathering spot for drinking beer and spending time with friends. In reality, Aling Lucing's sisig is located here, and it is here that the dish "sizzling pork sisig" was born.

Culture and Traditions

"Tigtigan Terakan Queng Dalan" is one of the most famous Kapampangan traditions, which has been passed down through generations and rekindles the Kapampangans' resilience during the explosion of Mount Pinatubo. Every final Friday and Saturday in October, it features dancing and singing in the streets of Balibago, Angeles City.

"When it comes to Kapampangans, it's impossible to overlook the fact that Pampanga is recognized as the "Culinary Capital of the Philippines," with a wide range of culinary delights ranging from traditional foods to desserts. Their talented chefs pass down their knowledge to the next generation of their families. In fact, Angeles City is home to the original branch of the famous Aling Lucing's sisig.

Pyestang Apu Mamacalulu is also a noteworthy occasion for Angelenos, which takes place on the final Friday of October and attracts a large number of devotees to the Holy Rosary Parish Church and the Apung Mamacalulu shrine to participate in the religious event. This is their method of honoring Christ's dead image as a symbol of hope in light of his resurrection.

The Hot Air Balloon Festival, held at Clarkfield, Angeles City every February, is one of Pampanga's most well-known yearly events. Many prominent musical bands are invited to perform live music, food vendors serve a range of appetizers and cuisines, and outdoor and art activities are also held during this festival.

The clothing or linen used during a baby's baptism is frequently framed and preserved in the living rooms of many Kapampangan families.

The practice of the man's family visiting the woman's family to obtain their consent or approval of the marriage is also common. The marriage is set up once they reach an agreement.
